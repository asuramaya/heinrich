--- READING
1. THE NULL HOLDS ON THE REAL BODY. The naive init measures 0.0131 against the
   predicted chance floor 0.0137 (0.96x). An untrained readout is ALREADY as
   'aligned' with the retrospective subspace as chance allows.

2. THE RESERVE DISSOLVES — MEASURED, NOT INFERRED. Arm C starts at frac 0.333
   (SW's hook, 24.4x chance) and decays 0.0727 @1k (5.3x) -> 0.0138 @5k (1.01x).
   By 5k the imposed geometry is INDISTINGUISHABLE FROM CHANCE. E4's verdict
   'init geometry does not survive training' is now a statement about weights.

3. TRAINING DOES NOT MERELY FORGET THE SUBSPACE — IT EVACUATES IT. Arms A, B and
   C2 all finish BELOW chance (0.0056 / 0.0059 / 0.0054 = 0.40-0.43x). The trained
   readout puts LESS energy in the retrospective directions than a random matrix
   would. It is not neutral about the lagged past; it turns away from it. This is
   the eviction story appearing in the READOUT's own geometry, where the campaign
   had only ever seen it in the state's information content.

4. MY OVER-CONCENTRATION HYPOTHESIS IS NOT SUPPORTED BY THIS EVIDENCE, and I will
   not let it stand on a correct prediction it did not earn. At 1k, arm C's
   effective rank (40.33) EQUALS naive's (40.27) — no rank collapse. But C's early
   penalty lived at steps 50-200, and the earliest saved checkpoint is 1k, by which
   time the loss curves had already converged. The regime where the effect existed
   was never captured. UNTESTED, not confirmed. What would settle it: checkpoints
   at 50/100/200 on arms C and C2.

5. FREE FINDING — ZEROED BUILDS LESS. Arm B ends with a SMALLER, LOWER-RANK block
   (norm 41.15, eff_rank 25.9) than naive (48.36, 33.4) at equal loss. B never has
   to unlearn anything, so it builds only what it needs. Independent support for
   SW's reading that the naive init's state-facing weights are noise the readout
   spends its early steps suppressing.
