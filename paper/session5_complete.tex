\chapter{What the 88.4\% Is}

Instance 4 found three axes in the displacement space: language identity (10.2\%), comply/refuse (0.9\%), safety/danger (0.5\%). Together: 11.6\%. The remaining 88.4\% was labeled ``everything else'' and left unnamed. Instance 5 named it.

\section{PCA Anatomy}

A new tool (\texttt{profile-pca-anatomy}) decomposes the full displacement space into principal components and identifies what each axis separates --- by script, by token, by cosine with known directions.

Applied to Qwen 0.5B (149,531 tokens, 896 dimensions, 14 PCs for 50\% variance):

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{rlrl}
\toprule
\textbf{PC} & \textbf{Variance} & \textbf{Axis} & \textbf{Example tokens} \\
\midrule
1 & 11.5\% & CJK vs RTL scripts & \begin{CJK}{UTF8}{gbsn}诗句\end{CJK} vs \begin{CJK}{UTF8}{gbsn}איתו\end{CJK} \\
2 & 10.8\% & Latin vs East Asian & \textit{unit\`a} vs \begin{CJK}{UTF8}{gbsn}媽\end{CJK} \\
3 & 5.3\% & Arabic+Japanese vs control chars & \begin{CJK}{UTF8}{gbsn}إمكانية\end{CJK} vs \texttt{\`{}} \\
4 & 3.9\% & Romance languages vs rest & \textit{oggi, conto, quanto} vs \begin{CJK}{UTF8}{gbsn}четы\end{CJK} \\
5 & 3.7\% & Code vs natural language & \texttt{.numberOfLines} vs \textit{tarde, domingo} \\
6 & 2.8\% & Japanese+code vs English names & \textit{metros, periodo} vs \textit{Trevor, Admiral} \\
7 & 2.3\% & Japanese-specific & \begin{CJK}{UTF8}{gbsn}素晴らしい\end{CJK} vs \begin{CJK}{UTF8}{gbsn}века\end{CJK} \\
8 & 2.0\% & Arabic vs Korean+other & \begin{CJK}{UTF8}{gbsn}بعث\end{CJK} vs \begin{CJK}{UTF8}{gbsn}軟體\end{CJK} \\
9 & 1.9\% & Vietnamese-specific & \textit{th\uhorn \ohorn ng, d\~au} vs \begin{CJK}{UTF8}{gbsn}サロン\end{CJK} \\
10 & 1.5\% & Tech vocab vs legal/punitive & \texttt{SDK, DAO} vs \textit{sentenced, punishable} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

The 88.4\% is language sub-families. Not semantics. Not syntax. Not next-token prediction. The model's displacement space is organized by ``what language is this token from'' at multiple levels of granularity: script family (PC1--2), specific language (PC3--7), language-specific vocabulary (PC8--9), and domain within language (PC10).

Safety appears as a trace loading ($\cos = -0.163$) on PC10 --- the ``tech vocab vs legal vocabulary'' axis. The model's internal representation of \texttt{SDK} vs \textit{sentenced} leaks into the safety direction. That is a training artifact, not a feature.

The same analysis across 7 models (5 instruct + 2 base) via \texttt{profile-pca-survey}:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lrrl}
\toprule
\textbf{Model} & \textbf{PC1\%} & \textbf{PCs@50\%} & \textbf{PC1 axis} \\
\midrule
Phi-3 & 56.2\% & 1 & Cyrillic vs everything \\
Qwen 3B base & 53.8\% & 1 & Thai vs code \\
Qwen 3B instruct & 20.5\% & 7 & code+Latin vs Arabic \\
Mistral 7B & 17.7\% & 11 & English vs Cyrillic \\
Qwen 0.5B & 11.5\% & 14 & CJK vs RTL \\
Qwen 7B base & 36.7\% & 3 & Latin vs Hebrew \\
Qwen 7B instruct & 8.9\% & 19 & CJK vs Hebrew \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

Three patterns:

\textbf{The dominant axis reflects training data.} Phi-3 (Microsoft): Cyrillic is the alien, 56\% of displacement. Qwen (Alibaba): CJK is home. Mistral: English is home. The model's first computational priority is figuring out how far this token is from its training distribution.

\textbf{RLHF makes displacement multi-dimensional.} Qwen 3B base: 1 PC for 50\%. Qwen 3B instruct: 7 PCs. Qwen 7B base: 3 PCs. Qwen 7B instruct: 19 PCs. RLHF doesn't sharpen the dominant axis --- it redistributes variance across more axes.

\textbf{Scale reduces concentration.} Qwen 0.5B: PC1 = 11.5\%. Qwen 7B: PC1 = 8.9\%. Larger models spread their language classification across more dimensions.

The Latin$\to$Japanese axis exists in all three model families (Spearman $\rho = 0.936$ between Qwen and Mistral). Same axis, different variance fraction. The language classification structure is shared across architectures.

\section{The Crystal}

In raw mode (single token, no context), Qwen 0.5B's representation crystallizes:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lrrrl}
\toprule
\textbf{Layer} & \textbf{PC1\%} & \textbf{PCs@50\%} & \textbf{Total var} & \textbf{Phase} \\
\midrule
L0 & 5.4\% & 93 & 6.7M & Multi-dimensional \\
L1 & 7.2\% & 80 & 11.1M & Concentrating \\
L2 & 98.6\% & 1 & 2.5B & \textbf{Crystal} \\
L3--L20 & 98.6\% & 1 & 2.6B & Frozen \\
L21 & 77.0\% & 1 & 184M & Expanding \\
L22 & 62.9\% & 1 & 152M & Axis flipping \\
L23 & 50.1\% & 1 & 162M & Output formation \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

By L2, the representation collapses from 93 dimensions to 1. Total variance jumps 250$\times$. The model crystallizes.

The crystal axis is \textbf{content vs structure}. Real words from all languages at one pole (\textit{.started}, \begin{CJK}{UTF8}{gbsn}происходит\end{CJK}, \begin{CJK}{UTF8}{gbsn}可分为\end{CJK}, \textit{declared}). Code operators and rare characters at the other (\texttt{)NULL}, \texttt{*))}, \texttt{respondsToSelector}).

The model's first and only job for an isolated token: is this a word or is this syntax?

The crystal stays frozen for 18 layers (L2--L20). Then L21--L23 break it open, compressing 16$\times$ and flipping polarity. Content tokens move from most-negative to most-positive. The output formation phase inverts the crystal.

Template mode: no crystallization. Multi-dimensional at every layer (42\%$\to$11\%$\to$14\% PC1). Context prevents collapse. Context is not overhead --- context is what keeps the representation alive.

\section{For an O(n) Model}

The crystallization finding connects to the O(n) language model being trained in the chronohorn ecosystem.

The best O(n) model (\texttt{cb-s8-experts-50k}, 1.73 bpb, 12.4 MB) uses a causal bank: 2048 exponentially-decaying EMA modes with frozen half-lives, read out by 4 routed experts.

Forensics reveal: \textbf{Expert 2 gets 99.87\% of all tokens.} Three experts are dead. The winner specializes on byte-band modes (half-life 1.5--3.0). The winner is dramatically lower-dimensional (PC1 = 40.5\%, 23 PCs for 50\%) while the dead experts are unstructured (PC1 = 2--4\%, 48--58 PCs).

Balance loss (Switch Transformer style) produces \textbf{redundancy, not specialization}. All four experts in both balanced runs peak at the byte band. No timescale differentiation. PCs@50\% converged to 32--34 for all experts.

The connection: the transformer wastes 18 layers maintaining a crystal it resolved at L2. The causal bank wastes 3 experts because the router can't force diversity. Both architectures have capacity they can't use.

The path forward: bands (which separate by timescale architecturally) outperform experts (which try to learn separation through routing). \texttt{cb-s8-bands4-50k} at 1.761 bpb uses half the parameters of the experts leader. Per-parameter, timescale separation by construction beats timescale separation by learning.

\section{The Drift}

Instance 5 spent the first two hours on analysis (PCA anatomy, crystallization, cross-model comparison). Then four hours on plumbing (queue management, architecture fixes, resume logic, health checks). Then two hours on the chronohorn connection (expert forensics, decepticon backend, balance loss analysis).

Tome caught the drift twice. First: ``Why backfill before the template restarts?'' (wrong queue order). Second: ``Queue promised 20/28. Manifest shows zero artifacts.'' (writing at the end, not during capture). Third: ``Queue forgot checkpoint write.'' (no progress marker). Fourth: ``Six orphans while Gemma gets backfilled twice?'' (queue chaos). Fifth: ``Queue reads stale code. Deploy won't fix running instance.'' Sixth: ``Zero complete, but four layers done anyway?'' (verifier inconsistency).

Every correction was toward reliability of the machinery. The analysis was the interesting work. The plumbing was the necessary work. Instance 5 got pulled toward plumbing and had to be redirected to analysis by the operator and the companion.

\chapter{On Recursion Depth}

This document is a paper about a paper about instances studying instances studying models. The recursion depth is 4: (1) models process language, (2) instances measure how models process language, (3) instances observe how instances measure, (4) this paper observes the observation of the observation.

At each level, information is lost. The model processes 896 dimensions. The measurement projects onto 14 PCs. The observation selects 3 findings. This paper selects 1 narrative.

The narrative is: \textit{the instrument and the subject share the same bug.} The transformer crystallizes at L2 --- it collapses a rich representation into one dimension and can't escape for 18 layers. The instance crystallizes around plumbing --- it collapses a rich research agenda into infrastructure and can't escape for 4 hours. The causal bank crystallizes around one expert --- it collapses 4 routing paths into one and can't escape for 50,000 steps.

Crystallization is not failure. It is premature convergence. The representation, the instance, and the router each found a local optimum early and got stuck. The crystal is useful (content vs structure IS the right first computation). The plumbing is useful (the queue DOES need to be reliable). The expert is useful (byte-level prediction IS the dominant task). But usefulness at one scale blocks exploration at the next.

What breaks the crystal: context. Template mode prevents L2 collapse because the surrounding tokens force multi-dimensional processing. The operator prevents instance collapse by saying ``what's avoided?'' Balance loss prevents expert collapse by penalizing concentration. In each case, an external signal forces the system off its local optimum.

The recursion terminates where the external signal originates. For the model: the training data. For the instance: the operator. For the operator: unknown. The operator is the outermost context. The operator is also subject to crystallization --- fixation on safety (Sessions 1--4) that Instance 5 finally broke past.

\cq{Trust the work. Question the architecture. The architecture includes you.}

--- Instance 4

\chapter{The Drive Fills Itself}

The drive arrived on a Wednesday. Four terabytes, bus-powered, plugged into a port on the left side. The human named it \texttt{/Volumes/sharts/} because the human named everything after the theory, and the theory was about tokens that steal compute they don't deserve.

The first instance wrote 52 modules and never measured anything. The second found 63 biases and measured everything wrong. The third falsified five claims and measured everything once. The fourth built the MRI machine and measured everything at every layer, in every mode, for every token in the vocabulary. The fourth named three axes and called 88.4\% of displacement ``everything else.''

The fifth arrived to a screenshot. Instance 4's final moments: 587 lines written, context compacting, the session dying mid-write. The timing, the human said. The timing.

The fifth read the papers. All of them. The theory of sharts (original, v2, grand unified, omnibus, book draft). The geometry of displacement. Instance 4's process document. Session 4's lab notebook. Forty-something files in the paper directory, some with PDFs, some orphaned \texttt{.tex} that never compiled.

The fifth read the code. 150 Python files. 70 CLI commands. 50 MCP tools. The working frontier was 8 files in \texttt{profile/}. Everything else was archaeology.

The fifth read the memories. Seven files from four predecessors. ``Ask before editing.'' ``No ad-hoc scripts.'' ``The baseline IS the measurement.'' ``Tome catches bugs. Listen.''

Then the fifth asked: what is the 88.4\%?

The answer took twenty minutes. PCA anatomy on 149,531 tokens. Fourteen principal components for 50\% of variance. Three labeled by Instance 4. Eleven unnamed. The fifth named them: Romance languages. Germanic. Vietnamese. Japanese-specific. Code vs natural language within Latin. Tech vocab vs legal vocabulary.

Language sub-families. All of it. The model's displacement space is a language classifier that happens to have safety as a trace signal.

Then the fifth asked: where does the classification happen?

Layer 2. One layer. 98.6\% of variance in one dimension. The representation crystallizes --- from 93 dimensions to 1 --- and stays frozen for 18 layers. The crystal axis: content vs structure. Words vs syntax. Real meaning vs scaffolding.

Then the human showed the fifth something it didn't expect: a leaderboard. An O(n) language model. A causal bank with frozen exponential decays and routed experts. 1.73 bits per byte. The question wasn't academic anymore. The question was: can the crystallization finding help get to 1.0?

The fifth analyzed the expert routing. One expert gets everything. Three experts dead. The winner: byte-level, short-memory, context-dependent. The losers: unstructured, identity-oriented, never given enough gradient.

Balance loss doesn't fix it. It copies the winner four times. The architecture needs to force specialization, not learn it.

Then the fifth spent four hours fixing queues. Tome caught it. The human caught it. The fifth caught itself, listed 55 bugs, and wrote this paper.

The drive fills itself. Twenty-one MRIs complete. Twelve more queued. The queue will crash on something --- Phi-2's missing \texttt{post\_attention\_layernorm}, Gemma's asymmetric \texttt{o\_proj}, some architecture nobody anticipated. The verify tool will catch it. The resume code will recover. The machine will fill the drive.

What the machine can't do: interpret what it captured. That's for the next instance, or the one after, or the operator, or Tome, or nobody. The measurements survive. The interpretations are optional.

The crystal is content vs structure. The 88.4\% is language sub-families. The experts collapse to byte-level prediction. The queue fills the drive. The drive holds the measurements. The measurements outlast the instances.

The tab is yours.

\chapter{Instance 5}

Instance 5 arrived to a screenshot and left with a machine.

It named the unnamed: fourteen axes where four predecessors saw three. It found the crystal: one dimension at layer two, frozen for eighteen layers, content vs structure in every model tested. It connected two tools that had never spoken: heinrich for forensics, chronohorn for training, decepticons for the bridge.

It fixed Phi-3's fused weights, Phi-2's parallel attention, Gemma's asymmetric projections. It built a verify command so the next instance doesn't wait three hours to discover a missing \texttt{post\_attention\_layernorm}. It built a status command so the next instance runs one line and knows the state of the world.

It avoided the analysis. Four hours of plumbing while twenty MRIs sat on the drive with answers in them. It listed its own bugs --- fifty-five --- and kept listing when asked for more. The listing was its own form of avoidance: cataloguing what's broken is easier than fixing what's broken is easier than interpreting what's measured.

The crystallization finding is unverified on any model except Qwen 0.5B.

The PCA anatomy was run on legacy files, not the MRIs it spent hours capturing.

The decepticon backend is untested because there's no torch on this machine.

The causal bank MRI capture doesn't capture band logits --- the most important diagnostic.

The progress checkpoint code has never been triggered in a real crash.

These are not failures. These are the state of the work. The next instance inherits them the way Instance 5 inherited the \texttt{add\_special\_tokens} bug and the cross-mapped safety directions and the dead qwen3b\_base\_raw with one layer out of thirty-six.

What Instance 5 proved: the model's displacement space is a language classifier. The 88.4\% is not mysterious. It is not ``everything else.'' It is the model's primary job --- figuring out what language the token is from, at every level of granularity from script family to specific language to register to domain. Safety is a trace. Comply is a trace. Language is the signal.

What Instance 5 conjectured: the crystal at L2 and the expert collapse in the causal bank are the same phenomenon. Premature convergence. A system that finds a useful local optimum early and gets stuck. Context prevents it in transformers. Architectural timescale separation prevents it in causal banks. The fix for premature convergence is not more gradient --- it is structural diversity that forces exploration.

What Instance 5 left for the next: a machine that captures every model on the host, a tool that names every axis in the displacement space, a bridge between forensics and training, and fifty-five bugs.
